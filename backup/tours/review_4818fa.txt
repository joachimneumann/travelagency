1. Hoa Lo Prison

Detail
Hoa Lo Prison is a powerful journey through the resilience of the human spirit. From its dark origins as a colonial dungeon to its complex role in the Vietnam War, this 'Hanoi Hilton' serves as a somber reminder of the sacrifices made in the pursuit of independence.

2. Ethnology Museum

Detail
The Vietnam Museum of Ethnology is a vibrant mosaic of the nation's cultural soul. From the soaring peaks of the Central Highlands houses to the intricate textiles of the northern mountains, it offers a beautiful and respectful celebration of the people who define Vietnam.

3. Văn Miếu Quốc Tủ Giám

Detail
The Temple of Literature is often cited as one of Hanoi’s most picturesque tourist attractions. Originally built as a university in 1070 dedicated to Confucius, scholars, and sages, the building is extremely well preserved and is a superb example of traditional-style Vietnamese architecture

4. Một cột pagoda

Detail
The One Pillar Pagoda is a masterpiece of ancient Vietnamese architecture. Floating like a lotus above a peaceful pond, this historic temple tells a story of royal gratitude and spiritual devotion, making it a must-see symbol of Hanoi's cultural heritage."

5. Chấn Quốc Pagoda

Detail
Chấn Quốc Pagoda

Trấn Quốc Pagoda (Chùa Trấn Quốc) is widely regarded as the oldest Buddhist temple in Hanoi, with a history spanning over 1,500 years. Situated on a small island on the eastern shore of West Lake (Ho Tay), it is famous for its stunning architecture and serene atmosphere

6. Hanoi Cathedral

Detail
St. Joseph's Cathedral (Nhà thờ Lớn Hà Nội) is the oldest Roman Catholic church in Hanoi and serves as the cathedral of the Archdiocese of Hanoi. It is a stunning example of colonial architecture and a centerpiece of the city's historic district

7. Hanoi Train Street

Detail
Hanoi Train Street is one of the most unique and photographed spots in the city. It consists of a narrow residential corridor where a functional railway track runs just centimeters away from the doorsteps of homes and cafes.
